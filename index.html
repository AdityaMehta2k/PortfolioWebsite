vibe
A Music Exploration Web-Application driven by Sentiment Analysis
Vibe is a sentiment analysis driven, music-exploration web application. The entire premise of the exploration is centred around a simple question - "How was your day today?"
Based on the users' answers it will generate a playlist that resonates with the nuanced emotions and experiences expressed in their input.
Vibe balances the intricacies of music recommendation with a refreshingly simple question that is void of any filters or predefined parameters. By asking users to describe their day without constraints, it captures genuine and diverse emotions, enriching the music exploration experience with authenticity and depth.

It employs a BERT model to detect keywords indicative of specific emotions, assigning them corresponding values. Once it evaluates the entire input, it determines the overarching sentiment value and communicates it to the front end; which in-turn dynamically loads the appropriate page and generates a playlist from the associated pool of songs.

Curated Pages
‚Äç‚Äç‚Äç‚ÄçA rainy Monday morning that beckons you to stay home wrapped in comfort; or those late, introspective nights swimming in regrets, questioning your direction and sense of purpose... Playlists made for surface-level emotions cant possibly fit in during these moments. Exploring different ideas with Curated Pages
Curated pages offer an avenue to infuse creativity into the experience and delve into various possibilities. For instance, I integrated a basic weather API to detect rainy conditions upon page load. If it's raining, Vibe triggers a flag to look out for phrases associated with a dull, grey rainy day; in-turn loading a specific playlist that caters to it. Curated pages enable such small but creative ideas to be layered on top of the main model to make for a more immersive music-exploration journey.
Feedback loop for users
The platform includes a song reporting feature, enabling users to flag songs that may not align well with the playlist. This action automatically generates an email to the admin account for review. As the project scales, a certain percentage threshold of reports from active users would determine when songs are automatically replaced, enhancing the overall quality and relevance of the playlists.
Conceptualising the future of vibe
Vibe was never meant to be a standalone app but a creative idea that could be used as an integrative feature for music-streaming platforms like Apple Music or Spotify. These platforms have extensive user data, which can optimise and elevate the project. A concept crafted along the same context is something I designed for my e-commerce project - Community Pages in Vibe. Users could suggest songs via Twitter, with the main account tweeting a hashtag for a specific sentiment, and users responding with their song choices. Although partially conceptual, the idea is to get people involved in curating playlists, something which will enhance user and community interaction while also feeding music in organically to pages.

" the forest of eden "

Leveraging Conversational AI to transform conversing and storytelling in games

Inspiration
My inspiration for "Forest of Eden" came rather organically; from my deep-rooted passion for the games I've played over the years. In open-world or story-focused games, immersion is everything. Many AAA titles do a remarkable job with world-building and character writing, crafting rich and engaging environments that draw players in. However, there's a common thread of disappointment when it comes to in-game conversing. These games often rely on a "three-choice dialogue" system, which more often than not feel robotic and lacklustre.

Witnessing this recurring flaw in an otherwise immersive experience sparked the idea for "Forest of Eden." The vision was to create a game where conversations are as dynamic and engaging as the world they take place in. By breaking free from the constraints of traditional dialogue mechanics, this project enables truly interactive and transformative storytelling.
Screenshot from Genshin Impact - An open world RPG game
Game development & design
Starting as my final year project for my undergrad, "Forest of Eden" was conceived with a commitment to constructing every aspect from the ground up. While a simple 2D game template could have sufficed, I was driven by two main motivations. Firstly, I wanted to delve into game design and development, exploring the intricacies of creating a game from its core elements. Secondly, I wanted to experience firsthand, the potential of conversational AI¬†in a full-fledged 3D game as close to the triple A titles as possible.

The complete game is built on Unity (Game Engine). Leveraging free Unity assets, I crafted an environment that captured the essence of exploration and interaction. A significant portion of my time was dedicated to seamlessly integrating Unity with the backend NLP models, a task that lacked any predefined blueprint. The resulting game features multiple NPCs and a short but compelling storyline woven into two elaborate scenes.
Question-Answering Model
as the centrepiece
The entire conversational aspect and all surrounding features of "Forest of Eden" revolved around a BERT question-answering model, which served as the centrepiece, driving a multitude of functionalities within the game. The overarching setup was straightforward: users would press an activation key in the game when near an NPC and be able to speak. A plugin tied to Unity would convert their speech to text, which would then be sent to the question-answering model via an API.

Given that both the game and the models were run locally, we decided to use socket programming as the bridge between Unity and the backend NLP models. The question-answering model would be fed a script, and upon receiving the user input, it would first recognise which NPC the player was interacting with and then switch the script to that particular NPC. The model would then evaluate the query based on the context and information available in the script and send the reply back to Unity.
Dynamic background music
powered by Sentiment Analysis
Background music is a crucial element in any game; it can elevate a great scene to a masterpiece and significantly enhance the overall exploration and in-game world-building. Subtly switching the music based on different in-game events is a detail that only a few games manage to get right. To build on this aspect, I leveraged sentiment analysis to dynamically adjust the background music. The sentiment analysis model would help identify the overarching tone of the conversation, allowing the game to subtly change the background music based on the emotional context. This feature not only enriched the immersive experience but also added a layer of emotional depth to the gameplay, making each interaction feel more nuanced and responsive.

There is a simple counter in place to keep track of the sentiment values attached to each response. When a certain threshold of continuous values is crossed, the backend would signal the game to switch to a more suitable soundtrack.
Quest Activation via conversing
& other additional features
Natural conversing in "Forest of Eden" offers a unique spin on quest activation, adding depth and immersion to the player experience. Instead of relying on traditional quest markers or predefined dialogue options, players must organically bring up topics during conversations with NPCs. Only then will NPCs reveal secret quests, creating a more authentic and engaging interaction. This approach encourages players to pay closer attention to their dialogues and fosters a deeper connection to the game world.
‚Äç
Players can learn about the history and culture of the game world through casual chats with NPCs, making the discovery of lore feel more organic and less like an information dump. Furthermore, updates and new content can be seamlessly woven into conversations, where specific NPCs might mention recent changes or new areas to explore.
A conceptual
plug and play framework
The entire backend of "Forest of Eden," encompassing all the NLP models, can be bundled up and offered as a curated service for game companies. This plug-and-play framework would allow developers to seamlessly integrate natural conversing into their own games with minimal effort. By simply feeding in the script and overarching lore, even a small indie game could incorporate dynamic and immersive conversations.

This framework offers a dual advantage: it presents a valuable monetisation opportunity for the project while simultaneously supporting and enriching the gaming community. Game developers, regardless of their resources or scale, could leverage this service to enhance their storytelling and character interactions, creating more engaging and immersive experiences for players. This approach not only democratises access to advanced conversational AI but also fosters innovation and creativity within the industry.
The GPT¬†buff
Considering that "Forest of Eden" was completed before ChatGPT took the internet by storm, the potential of integrating GPT API for the backend really intrigued me. Once I linked the project with the GPT API, the possibilities felt endless. The advanced capabilities of GPT allowed me to cultivate actual social relationships between NPCs and intertwine them with the lore of the story.

For instance, I created an NPC who would occasionally lie about things, requiring players to complete a side quest to gain his trust. In another example, I had two NPCs who were childhood friends and knew each other's tendencies. They would share certain information, so if you talked to one of them about a quest, the other would have a good chance of finding out what happened. The integration of GPT added a new layer of immersion, making the game world feel more alive and interconnected. Considering the aforementioned, I genuinely feel that we're only a small step away from seeing a huge transformation in the gaming space, which would be enable and powered by Conversational AI

As for the future of the project, its quite literally parallel to the advancements made in NLP. Now with generative AI becoming both, more enhanced and accessible, its only a short matter of time till the AAA studios start harnessing its powers.

An elaborate research summary on Mobile Edge Computing (MEC)

I authored a comprehensive research summary synthesizing insights from over 15 papers on Mobile Edge Computing (MEC). This summary highlights how MEC is transforming low-latency communication by decentralising data processing, bringing it closer to mobile users, and reducing latency. MEC supports various applications, including autonomous vehicles, AR/VR, smart cities, and healthcare, by providing real-time data processing and enhanced reliability. This research significantly increased my interest in the field of edge computing, emphasizing its potential to revolutionize communication and application performance.

Leveraging NLP to gauge consumer perception of rental products

As part of my RAC tenure, I employed DistilBERT for sentiment analysis on consumer reviews to evaluate the overall perception of laptop rentals over their last 5 quarters. This approach yielded a commendable 94% accuracy rate, underscoring the effectiveness of using advanced NLP models in understanding customer feedback. To further simplify data interpretation, I integrated topic detection techniques, which highlighted both positive and negative focal points in the reviews. This analysis provided actionable insights for product improvements, enabling us to better meet customer expectations. Additionally, the analysis uncovered certain product placement anomalies, prompting the strategic removal of two underperforming laptop models from student recommendations.

A conceptual conversational AI-driven architecture for theme parks

A culmination of the various technologies I've explored and used extensively throughout my projects, this project aims to be a ¬†full-fledged conceptual architecture curated for places like malls and theme parks. This architecture centers around conversational AI, designed specifically for these environments. Users can connect to a simple smartphone application featuring a virtual assistant that facilitates natural conversation for recommendations, descriptions, and more. The entire architecture is supported by Mobile Edge Computing (MEC), which ensures low-latency responses, making interactions seamless. Additionally, edge nodes would process real-time data, such as footfall analytics for different rides and shops. This data can be used for backend analytics or integrated into the mobile app to inform users about crowded areas. These are preliminary ideas, with a detailed research paper currently in progress.
